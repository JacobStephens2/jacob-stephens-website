Preface

 

What is my purpose in reviewing my education? I want to remember the content, continue to refine the skills presented and began to be taught in me. There’s much to learn beyond what I’ve been taught in school, and much of the schooling itself to evaluate, but I think that first reviewing what is there and going on from that point will prove helpful.

            How can I decide what materials are important to keep or have? First, which are mine? If they’re mom and dad’s, I ought to take extra consideration in making them part of my collection. This collection doesn’t need to travel, because currently I don’t live anywhere else. Though I’d like my access to things I don’t have to be through internet, because then I have many fewer items to find access to when moving to a new place. However, I could take the books that BCPL has in book form, but not electronically, and put those in a box. How do I decide on Amadeus? On the Bhagavad Gita? On Redwall? I want access to the ideas and practices of these classes, and I want access to the relationships and people, as much as possible.

            However, what if I go to China? Or some other place where my internet connection is limited? What if I can no longer access these resources? I will be okay, however, having the a non internet requiring copy would make possible my review of them without requiring internet connection. I am still okay with digital editions, so long as they don’t require internet to access.
